Core Admission Requirements

Before applying to State Colleges of Education (36 States), ensure you meet these fundamental criteria. Falling short on any of these can lead to automatic disqualification during screening.

  • JAMB Expectation: You must score a minimum of 180 to write the Post-UTME. Highly competitive courses demand higher scores.
  • O'Level Results: Minimum of 5 Credit passes (including English Language and Mathematics). Double check if your department accepts two sittings. View O'Level breakdown for your course →
  • Subject Combination: Your chosen JAMB subjects MUST perfectly match the official JAMB brochure requirements for State Colleges of Education (36 States). Verify your exact JAMB subject combination here →
  • First Choice: Most institutions require them to be your first choice.
  • Age Requirement: Candidates must be at least 16 years of age by October 31st of the admission year.

Departmental Cut-Off Insights

Do not confuse the General Cut-off with the Departmental Cut-off. Meeting the general cut-off only allows you to buy the form. To actually gain admission, you need to meet the specific requirements of your chosen department.

Medicine, Law, Pharmacy, Nursing 250 - 280+ Safe Score
Engineering, Computer Science, Architecture 220 - 250+ Safe Score
Social Sciences, Physical Sciences 200 - 220+ Safe Score
Education, Arts, Agriculture 180 - 200+ Safe Score

Post-UTME & Screening Format

Your final aggregate score is heavily dependent on this exam.

Test Format

A strictly timed Computer-Based Test (CBT) or Online Screening. Speed and accuracy are vital.

What to Prepare For

Questions usually cover your core JAMB subjects. In some cases, General Paper and Current Affairs may be included.

Aggregate Calculation

Typically, your aggregate is computed by combining your JAMB score percentage and Post-UTME score. A high Post-UTME can save a low JAMB score.

Calculate your aggregate score here

Course Competitiveness

Apply strategically based on your UTME performance. Choose your battle wisely.

Highly Competitive (Risk is High)

Medicine, Pharmacy, Law, Accounting, Nursing. These courses attract thousands of applicants for very limited slots. You need an outstanding aggregate to qualify.

Moderately Competitive

Economics, Mass Communication, Architecture, Computer Science. Solid JAMB scores and a good POST-UTME performance will guarantee admission here.

Less Competitive (Safer Bets)

Education Courses, Botany, Zoology, Agricultural Sciences. If you have a lower score, these courses offer a much more realistic chance of gaining admission.

Admission Strategy & Advice

Should You Apply?

If you scored above 180, yes. However, if your target is Medicine and you scored 190, you must change your course before Post-UTME registration closes to avoid wasting your slot.

Catchment Advantage

As a Federal or State University, they often use the Catchment Area policy. Candidates from specific catchment states may have a slightly lower cut-off threshold.

Smart Alternatives

If your JAMB score is low for Law, consider International Studies. If low for Medicine, switch to Anatomy or Physiology. A strategic switch is better than staying unadmitted.

Common Admission Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong Subject Combination: Choosing wrong JAMB subjects automatically disqualifies you. The portal will filter you out without human intervention. Don't make this mistake! Verify your subject combination now.
  • Awaiting Results Negligence: Using "Awaiting Result" is fine, but failing to upload your O'Level to the JAMB CAPS portal once it is released means you will not be offered admission.
  • Late Post-UTME Preparation: Waiting for the form to be released before studying. Top scorers begin practicing past questions months in advance.
