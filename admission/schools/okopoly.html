Core Admission Requirements

Before applying to OKOPOLY, ensure you strictly meet these fundamental criteria. OKOPOLY is known for a very strict verification process.

  • JAMB Expectation: You MUST score a minimum of 200 to register for the Post-UTME screening. Any score below 200 outrightly disqualifies you.
  • O'Level Results: Minimum of 5 Credit passes (including English Language and Mathematics) obtained in ONLY ONE (1) SITTING. OKOPOLY does not accept two sittings. View O'Level breakdown for your course →
  • Subject Combination: Your chosen JAMB subjects MUST perfectly match the official JAMB brochure requirements for OKOPOLY. Verify your exact JAMB subject combination here →
  • First Choice: OKOPOLY must be your absolute first choice in JAMB. They do not process second choice applications.
  • Age Requirement: Candidates must be at least 16 years old by October 31st of the admission year.

Departmental Cut-Off Insights

OKOPOLY's aggregate is out of 100%. Securing simply the JAMB score of 200 only lets you write the Post-UTME. You need to hit a strong final aggregate required for your specific department.

Post-UTME & Screening Format

OKOPOLY conducts a highly competitive and fast-paced computer-based aptitude test.

Test Format

A rigorous Computer-Based Test (CBT) covering Mathematics, English, and General Paper. Speed is crucial as you have very limited time (often 40 questions in 30 mins).

What to Prepare For

Unlike JAMB subjects, OKOPOLY tests all candidates heavily on General Mathematics, Use of English, and Current Affairs / Logical Reasoning regardless of their course.

Aggregate Calculation

JAMB score (divided by 8) = 50 marks. Post-UTME exam = 30 marks. O'Level Grades = 20 marks. Total = 100 marks.

Course Competitiveness

Apply strategically based on your UTME performance. Here is how competitive OKOPOLY courses get.

Highly Competitive (Risk is High)

Medicine, Pharmacy, Law, Accounting, Nursing, Mass Communication. You need exceptionally high O'Level grades (mostly A's) and 280+ in JAMB to confidently compete here.

Moderately Competitive

Economics, Architecture, Computer Science, Engineering. Solid JAMB scores and a great POST-UTME showing will put you in a very good position to win admission.

Less Competitive (Safer Bets)

Education Courses, Adult Education, Botany, Zoology, Arts. Courses with generally lower applicant ratios, providing a safer path to entry if you've hit the 200 score mark.

Admission Strategy & Advice

Assess Your O'Levels Early

Because O'Level grades make up 20% of your total aggregate (A1=4.0, B2=3.6, etc.), having multiple C's realistically weakens your chance for top-tier competitive courses.

Catchment Advantage

OKOPOLY utilizes catchment. Beneficiaries must legally claim origin from states in the South-West region (Lagos, Ogun, Oyo, Osun, Ondo, Ekiti) for a marginally lower cut-off criteria.

Practice Smartly

Preparation must prioritize speed. You will answer questions much faster manually if you rely heavily on OKOPOLY-specific past questions that train your ability on logical reasoning.

Common Admission Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong Subject Combination: Choosing wrong JAMB subjects automatically disqualifies you. The portal will filter you out without human intervention. Don't make this mistake! Verify your subject combination now.
  • Combining O'Level Results: Submitting two sittings will cause disqualification as OKOPOLY only accepts ONE sitting.
  • Failure to Upload O'Level to CAPS: Registration is null if your O'Level isn't fully synched on the JAMB portal.
