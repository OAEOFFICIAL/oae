JAMB is over — What next?

Find out your next steps, check your admission chances, and discover the best path to secure your admission this year.

What To Do After JAMB

After writing JAMB, many students feel confused about what comes next. Follow these simple steps to stay on track and increase your chances of gaining admission this year.

1

Check Your Result

Confirm your JAMB score and understand where you stand. Your result will determine your next move, so make sure you know your exact score.

2

Evaluate Your Score

Compare your score with cut-off marks for your desired course and school. This helps you know if you're on track or if you need to make adjustments.

3

Choose School or Course

Based on your score, decide whether to stick with your current choice or consider a smarter alternative to improve your admission chances.

4

Prepare for Post-UTME

Start preparing early for your school's screening or exam. The earlier you begin, the higher your chances of success.

Is Your Score Good?

Not sure what your JAMB score means? Find your score range below and see the best steps you can take to secure admission this year.

180 – 200

You still have good chances, but you need to be strategic. Consider less competitive courses or schools, and stay updated on institutions with flexible admission requirements.

200 – 250

You are in a strong position for many schools. Focus on preparing well for Post-UTME and make smart choices when selecting your course and institution.

250+

Excellent score! You have a high chance of gaining admission into competitive courses and top universities. Stay focused and prepare properly for the next stage.

How To Choose The Right School

Choosing the right school is just as important as your JAMB score. Use these key factors to make a smart decision and improve your chances of gaining admission.

Federal vs State vs Private

Federal universities are usually more competitive, while state universities may have slightly lower competition. Private universities offer easier admission but can be more expensive. Choose what fits your situation best.

Course Competition

Some courses are highly competitive and require very high scores. If your score is not strong enough, consider related or less competitive courses to increase your chances.

Location & Strategy

Consider the location of the school and your overall admission strategy. Sometimes choosing a less competitive location can improve your chances of getting admitted.

Should You Change Your Course or School?

Not sure if you should change your course or institution? Here's how to make the right decision based on your score and admission chances.

When To Change

Consider changing your course or institution if your JAMB score is far below the required cut-off for your chosen course or school. Making a smart change early can significantly improve your chances of gaining admission.

When Not To Change

If your score meets or is close to the required cut-off, you may not need to change anything. Focus on preparing well for Post-UTME instead of making unnecessary changes.

Smart Alternatives

If your preferred course is too competitive, consider related courses with lower competition. You can also explore schools with more flexible admission requirements to increase your chances.

Prepare for Post-UTME

After JAMB comes the next important stage — Post-UTME or screening. Understanding what to expect early gives you a strong advantage over other candidates.

What To Expect

Post-UTME varies by school. Some universities conduct CBT exams, while others use screening based on your documents and JAMB score. You must be prepared for either format.

CBT or Screening

CBT involves answering questions on a computer under time conditions. Screening may involve checking your results, documents, and JAMB score. Know your school's method early so you can prepare properly.

Practice & Preparation

Consistent practice improves your chances of success. Soon on OAE, you will be able to access Post-UTME practice questions and mock tests to prepare effectively.

Mistakes To Avoid After JAMB

Many students lose admission opportunities not because of their score, but because of avoidable mistakes. Learn and avoid these errors.

Waiting Too Long

Delaying your Post-UTME preparation or ignoring admission updates can reduce your chances. Always act early once JAMB is over.

Wrong Course Choice

Choosing a course without checking requirements or competition level can lead to admission failure. Always compare your score with course requirements before deciding.

Ignoring Updates

Admission information changes quickly. Schools release cut-off marks, Post-UTME forms, and deadlines regularly. Ignoring updates can make you miss important opportunities.

Scored Low? Don't Panic

A low JAMB score does not mean the end of your admission journey. There are still many smart and realistic options available to you.

Alternative Schools

Many universities have flexible admission opportunities depending on your course and score. You can still gain admission into less competitive schools or related courses with lower cut-off marks.

Polytechnics

Polytechnics are a strong alternative path. They offer quality education and practical training, and many students gain admission more easily compared to universities.

Other Options

You can also consider options like change of institution, diploma programmes, JUPEB, or pre-degree programmes to improve your chances of entering your desired course later.

Stay Updated

Admission updates change frequently. To avoid missing important information, you need to stay updated regularly.
