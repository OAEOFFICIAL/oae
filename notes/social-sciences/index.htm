What is Social Sciences?

Social Sciences encompass the systematic study of human society, behavior, and the institutions that organize social life. These disciplines examine economic systems, geographical patterns, political structures, historical events, and human societies to understand how people interact and organize themselves.

Whether exploring economic principles, analyzing geographical patterns, understanding government systems, studying historical events, or examining human societies, Social Sciences disciplines provide insights into the fundamental forces that shape our world and prepare students for careers in economics, geography, politics, international relations, journalism, and social policy.
