Understanding Kinematics: The Equations of Motion

What is Kinematics?

Kinematics is the branch of mechanics that describes the motion of points, bodies, and systems of bodies without considering the forces that cause the motion. It strictly focuses on velocity, acceleration, displacement, and time.

When studying motion, many students get confused by the four standard equations. The secret to mastering kinematics is not memorizing the formulas, but understanding what each variable represents and knowing when to use which equation.

Real-World Analogy: The Road Trip

Imagine you are driving from Lagos to Ibadan. Your starting speed is your Initial Velocity (u). If you step on the gas pedal, you are experiencing Acceleration (a). Your speed when you arrive is your Final Velocity (v). The distance between the cities is Displacement (s), and the duration of the trip is Time (t).

Step-by-Step Problem Solving Breakdown

To solve any motion calculation question in JAMB or WAEC, simply follow this 3-step framework:

  • List the Knowns: Read the question and write out the variables given. (e.g., "A car starts from rest" means `u = 0`).
  • Identify the Unknown: What is the question asking for? Is it distance (`s`) or final velocity (`v`)?
  • Select the Missing-Variable Equation: Look at your list. Which of the 4 variables is completely missing (not given and not asked for)? Pick the equation that ignores that variable.

Common Exam Pitfall

When a question says "brakes are applied" or "comes to a halt", your Final Velocity (v) is 0, and your Acceleration (a) must be negative (deceleration). Forgetting the minus sign is the #1 reason students fail these questions!

Summary

Always draw a mental picture of the motion before picking a formula. Master the abbreviations (u, v, a, s, t), and remember that dropping the negative sign during deceleration will ruin your final answer.
